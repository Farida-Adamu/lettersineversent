February 12, 2025

You are not behind

To my twenty-five-year-old self,

I know how it looks from where you're standing. Everyone else seems to know something you don't, to have arrived somewhere you haven't. They haven't. They're just further along in their particular confusion.

You will spend a lot of time in cities where you don't speak the language, eating alone, becoming someone who is good at being alone without being lonely. This will be one of the most useful things that happens to you.

Also: you will get the thing you want. Just not yet, and not in the shape you're imagining. That part will be better.

— you, at 31

January 20, 2025

To the friend who faded,

I still think about the last time we spoke — really spoke — and how neither of us knew it was the last time. I think that's how most endings work. Not with a door closing but with a silence that stretches until you forget there was ever sound.

I don't blame you. I'm not sure I even miss you specifically. I miss the version of myself that existed in your company. She was funnier. Less careful.

If you ever read this: I hope you're sleeping better. I hope someone is asking you the right questions.

— F

March 4, 2025

Dear Metz,

I thought leaving would feel like relief. But it felt like putting down something heavy and then spending the next few weeks wondering why my hands still remembered the shape of it.

I'm writing this from a new arrondissement now. The light comes in differently here — earlier in the morning, through a window that faces east instead of west. I keep expecting to feel at home and then remembering that I chose this, which should help, and mostly doesn't.

I think what I miss is not you exactly. It's the version of me that still believed the move would fix something.

— F
